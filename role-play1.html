Role Play Exercise

Topic: Calling Technical Support for Biomedical Equipment

Objective

Practice making a professional phone call in English to report a technical issue and request assistance with biomedical equipment troubleshooting.

Roles

  • Hospital Technician: Describes the issue and answers questions from support.
  • Support Representative: Responds, asks for details, and gives troubleshooting steps.

After one call, switch roles so each learner practices both sides.

Instructions

  1. Read your assigned scenario below.
  2. Use the example prompts to start your call.
  3. Focus on professional tone and precise vocabulary.
  4. End by summarizing the next steps clearly with your partner.

Grammar Focus

During your conversation, decide whether to use the Present Perfect or the Simple Past depending on the situation.

  • Present Perfect – use it for actions connected to the present moment (“We’ve already replaced the tubing.”)
  • Simple Past – use it for completed, time‑specific events (“We replaced the tubing yesterday.”)

Try to include both tenses naturally as you describe what checks have been done and what happened earlier(specific finished time).

Useful Vocabulary

outlet – prise électrique
power supply – alimentation
error code – code d’erreur
sensor – capteur
alarm – alarme
shutdown – arrêt
reboot – redémarrer
flow sensor – capteur de débit
self‑test – auto‑test
cable connection – connexion de câble

Scenarios

1. Ventilator: The ventilator will not start after a power outage. The backup ventilator is working, but this one needs to be operational again.
2. Defibrillator: The defibrillator fails its self‑test. The screen shows an unknown error code and a low battery warning, even after charging.
3. Pulse Oximeter: The readings fluctuate between patients. The sensor cable may be damaged or not properly connected.
4. Infusion Pump: Displays error code E103 – flow sensor fault. Tubing has been replaced, but the alarm continues to sound.
5. Patient Monitor: The screen flickers and does not reboot even after checking the power connection.

Example Prompts

Hello, this is [Name] from [Hospital/Department]. We’re having a problem with our [Device Name]. Could you help us troubleshoot it?
Hi, I’m calling about an error message on our [Equipment]. It shows code [XXX]. Could you tell me what it means?
Good afternoon, the device won’t start even after following the manual’s instructions. What do you recommend?
We’ve done the standard checks, but the issue continues. Could you guide us through the next troubleshooting steps?
We may need on-site service if this can’t be fixed remotely. What information do you need to schedule a visit?
Reflection Questions
  • Was the problem described clearly and logically?
  • Did you use the correct tense (present perfect or simple past) when describing past actions?
  • Did both sides confirm key information: model, serial number, error code?
  • Were next actions summarized accurately and politely?
